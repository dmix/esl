Minimum Requirements

to teach with EIE

Candidates interested in teaching in China in one of our programs must have completed a bachelor’s degree at an accredited university and have a minimum of two years’ related teaching experience. Candidates must be native, proficient English speakers, or have come to age since at least age 13 in a country where English is the official language. Candidates must have a thorough grasp of English spelling, grammar, vocabulary and writing ability.

Once hired, prospective teachers will receive in-depth information on how to obtain the proper documentation, including a Z visa, that is required for them to work at EIE. In order to apply for your Z visa, you will need a worker permit notification, which EIE will assist you in obtaining from the China Foreign Expert Bureau. In order for us to obtain the worker’s permit notification for you, you will be required to send:

  • A detailed resume reviewing your education including high school attended and work experience, labelled by the corresponding month and year
  • A visa photo
  • All degrees and certificates*
  • Photo page of your passport (your passport’s expiration date must be 12 months’ or longer from the date of your application)
  • References from employers in positions related to teaching
  • Completed medical form
  • Criminal record check*

* Degree and criminal background check needs to be notarized

In order to receive a worker’s permit, unless you have majored in English or Education, you must have had two full years’ of teaching experience after the completion of your bachelor’s degree.

Once you have obtained a Z visa, it must be converted to a residence permit within 30 days’ of your arrival in China, which will be valid for the rest of the duration of your contract. EIE will assist you in renewing your residence permit should you wish to renew your contract with us.
