Nadia Guo

Nadia Guo has been with Forte Defence since autumn of 2014 while she was completing her final year of law school at the University of Toronto’s Faculty of Law. It was through her study of the criminal law and its complicated relationship with civil liberties that she found her destined nook in the legal sphere. The unrelenting human emotion and moral ambiguities that define the conflicts in criminal law casebooks drove Ms. Guo’s unyielding fervour for this type of work. Her interests in outcasts dwelling at the peripheries of the law, philosophical dramas, and transgressions, be they legal or social, are aptly fitting. Ms. Guo is now completing her articles with Forte Defence under the direct supervision of Mr. Forte.

Ms. Guo obtained her Juris Doctor from Osgoode Hall Law School, where she completed her first two years of studies. She maintained a strong focus on criminal law throughout law school, exposing herself to a diverse array of issues endemic to criminal justice. This included courses on the prevalence of mental illness among the criminally accused, the use of restorative justice and other rehabilitative techniques rather than the current over-reliance on incarceration, the unique nature of homicide offences, and the (mis)uses of scientific evidence in the trial setting. Ms. Guo excelled above average in her coursework, earning the Kenneth Gibson Morden Memorial Prize in Evidence, as well as achieving the top grade in the Law and Literature seminar.

Ms. Guo gained exposure to casework from early on in her law school career, volunteering at Osgoode’s Community and Legal Aid Services Programme (CLASP), where she worked on summary conviction matters and did routine court appearances for low-income clients from the Jane and Finch community. Her interest in access to justice and public interest-oriented law continued to develop during her semester at Downtown Legal Services, another student-run legal aid clinic at the University of Toronto. At DLS, Ms. Guo achieved favourable results for her clients, ranging from full withdrawal of all charges, to peace bonds and absolute discharges. Ms. Guo delivered personalized attention to all her clients, and made it her top concern that clients were fully informed of their legal options at every step of the process. By working closely with members of the community afflicted by socioeconomic disadvantages, Ms. Guo gained valuable insight into the unique challenges faced by individuals from this demographic who come into conflict with the law.

Ms. Guo also gained valuable experience working in Canada’s burgeoning medical cannabis industry during her first year summer at a firm specializing in medical marijuana-related cases. There, Ms. Guo grew acquainted with some of the major corporate players seeking licenced producer status under the Marihuana for Medical Purposes Regulations.

Ms. Guo enrolled in Osgoode’s Criminal Intensive Programme in her second year and interned at Canada’s largest criminal law firm, where she worked on complex files involving child pornography, impaired driving, robbery, homicides, conspiracy, firearms, drugs, and sexual assault charges. She had the valuable opportunity to observe different types of trials in both the Ontario Court of Justice as well as the Ontario Superior Court. She also watched appeal hearings at the Court of Appeal, and through all this was able to gain the rare privilege of gleaning courtroom advocacy techniques from some of Canada’s best defence lawyers. Ms. Guo spent her second summer at a renowned criminal law practice where she continued to garner familiarity with courthouses across the Greater Toronto Area, while further cultivating her legal draftsmanship and research ability.

Ms. Guo completed her Honours Bachelor of Arts at the University of Toronto in English Literature and Cinema Studies. Her affection for literature (her favourite novelists are experimental modernists like Woolf and Faulkner), cinema (lately: Xavier Dolan, eternally: Wong Kar-wai), and the arts in general serve as a healthy foundation for the more rigorous and disciplined study of law. Ms. Guo’s legal advocacy is enriched by the poeticism of her studies in liberal arts, as starkly demonstrated by her mastery of rhetoric and syntax.

Ms. Guo’s authoritative confidence and intellect drive her work both on paper and in the courtroom. Her sharp criticism brings a refreshing perspective to every legal challenge, and her dry wit charms colleagues and clients alike. She is outspoken about and denounces police brutality, government corruption, and race- sex-, and class-based profiling. Ms. Guo understands that her legal education is a privilege, and is unafraid to use it to assist underdogs who have every odd stacked against them. Though petite in frame and young in her years, her larger-than-life reputation for bold doggedness will serve her well as her career blossoms.

Ms. Guo is fascinated by darknet markets, Tor networks, cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin, and digital forensics. She hopes to specialize in defending cybercrime cases, and aspires to one day soon be known as Canada’s leading darknet lawyer. This is especially so as online black market contraband transactions proliferate steadily. She is a strong advocate of privacy rights and supports the increased usability of encryption-based technologies to protect digital privacy.

Ms. Guo also unequivocally supports the decriminalization and perhaps even legalization of all drugs. Empirical evidence has shown time and time again that the war on drugs and the criminalization of drug offences has proven to be counter-intuitive, needlessly expensive, and entails costly social damage. Ms. Guo firmly believes that the problems caused by substance abuse are better off addressed by expert health practitioners, and not the clumsy, ill-fitted hammer of the penal system. She plans to focus her legal career on advocating for sensible, holistic, and rehabilitative-centred solutions to substance abuse.
